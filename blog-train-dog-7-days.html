How to Train Your Dog in 7 Days

✍️ Paw School Team 📅 June 2025 ⏱️ 8 min read 🐕 All Breeds

Think dog training takes months of expensive lessons? Think again. This proven 7-day method works for any breed, any age — using nothing but treats, consistency, and 15 minutes a day.

We've all been there — your dog pulls the leash until your arm hurts, jumps on every guest, ignores you completely at the park. It feels hopeless. But here's the truth: most dogs aren't badly behaved — they're just undertrained.

This guide will walk you through exactly what to do, day by day, for 7 days. No expensive trainer needed. Just 15 minutes a day, high-value treats, and the techniques in this guide.

🎒 Before You Start — What You Need

Your Training Starter Kit
  • High-value treats (small, soft, smelly — hot dogs work great)
  • A clicker or a consistent "Yes!" marker word
  • A 6-foot leash (not retractable)
  • A quiet space with no distractions
  • 15 minutes of focused time per day
  • Patience and a positive attitude 😊

Day by Day — Your Training Plan

D1
Day One
Sit & Eye Contact 👀

This is the foundation of everything. A dog that makes eye contact with you is a dog that's paying attention to you.

  • Hold a treat at your dog's nose, then slowly move it up over their head
  • Their bottom will naturally lower — the moment it touches the floor, say "Yes!" and give the treat
  • Repeat 10 times, then add the word "Sit" just before they do it
  • For eye contact: hold a treat near your eye, wait for your dog to look at your face, then reward
Pro Tip: Keep sessions to 5 minutes max for puppies. Short and fun beats long and boring every time.
D2
Day Two
Stay Command ✋

Stay is one of the most important safety commands your dog can learn. Start small — just 3 seconds — and build up slowly.

  • Ask your dog to sit, then open your palm toward them like a stop sign
  • Say "Stay" and wait 3 seconds, then reward while they're still sitting
  • If they move, calmly reset — no punishment, just try again
  • Gradually increase: 5 seconds → 10 seconds → 30 seconds over the week
Pro Tip: Always release with a word like "Okay!" or "Free!" so your dog knows when the stay is over.
D3
Day Three
Come When Called 📣

"Come" is the most important command for your dog's safety. Practice this every single day for the rest of your dog's life.

  • Start indoors with zero distractions
  • Crouch down, open your arms, say your dog's name + "Come!" in a happy voice
  • When they reach you, throw a PARTY — big treats, praise, petting
  • NEVER call your dog to you for something unpleasant (like a bath)
Pro Tip: Use a long 15-30 foot training line outdoors before trusting off-leash recall. It saves lives.

D4
Day Four
Loose Leash Walking 🚶

Leash pulling is the #1 complaint of dog owners. The fix is simple, but requires consistency. Every single walk is a training session.

  • The moment your dog pulls — STOP. Become a statue
  • Only move forward when the leash has slack
  • Reward your dog every few steps when they walk nicely beside you
  • Change direction randomly — keep them focused on YOU
Pro Tip: A front-clip no-pull harness reduces pulling by 70% immediately while you train. Game changer.
D5
Day Five
Leave It Command 🚫

"Leave it" can save your dog's life — from eating something dangerous on a walk or leaving the cat alone.

  • Hold a treat in a closed fist. Let your dog sniff and paw at it
  • The SECOND they back away, say "Yes!" and give a treat from your OTHER hand
  • Repeat until they look at your face instead of your fist
  • Then add the words "Leave it" just before they would normally sniff
Pro Tip: Always reward with a DIFFERENT treat than what you asked them to leave. This teaches them leaving = something better.
D6
Day Six
Stop Jumping Up 🙅

Jumping is attention-seeking behavior. The solution: jumping = zero attention. Four paws on floor = all the love.

  • When your dog jumps — turn your back completely, cross your arms, say nothing
  • The moment all four paws hit the floor — immediately reward with treats and praise
  • Ask every guest to do the same — inconsistency undoes all your work
  • Teach "Off" by saying it as they land, then reward
Pro Tip: Ask your dog to "Sit" when greeting guests. It's physically impossible to sit and jump at the same time.
D7
Day Seven
Socialization & Real World 🌍

Now it's time to take everything you've practiced and test it in the real world with distractions.

  • Practice "Sit" and "Stay" in a new location (park, parking lot, friend's house)
  • Practice "Come" with mild distractions nearby
  • Expose your dog to new sights, sounds, and people — always pair with treats
  • Keep sessions short — 10 minutes is enough in a stimulating environment
Pro Tip: If your dog can't focus, you've moved too fast. Go back to an easier environment and rebuild confidence.

What Happens After Day 7?

Congratulations — your dog now knows the 7 core commands! But training doesn't stop here. The best-behaved dogs are the ones whose owners practice daily, even if just for 5 minutes.

🔑 The Golden Rule of Dog Training

Every interaction with your dog is training. How you respond when they jump, pull, or bark is either reinforcing bad habits or building good ones. Consistency is everything — not intensity.

Week 2 and beyond, work on:

  • Distance and duration on "Stay" command
  • Off-leash recall in a fenced area
  • Advanced commands like "Down," "Place," and "Heel"
  • Fun tricks to bond and keep your dog mentally sharp
